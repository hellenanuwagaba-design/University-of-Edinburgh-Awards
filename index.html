Digital Skills Specialists Edinburgh Award

The Digital Skills Specialists Edinburgh Award is designed to help students develop and showcase their digital skills while making a real impact. Running from October to March, the program guides participants through identifying the skills they want to improve, developing them through workshops and self-directed learning, and applying them in a practical project. Students take part in mandatory group sessions, complete 16 hours of self-directed learning, attend workshops, and carry out a project that demonstrates their new skills. Throughout the award, reflection and sharing your progress are key, helping you understand your strengths, track your growth, and confidently communicate your achievements to future employers.

Sustainability Leadership Edinburgh Award

The Sustainability Leadership Edinburgh Award helps students develop leadership skills for climate action and sustainability. It provides training in change management, critical thinking, systems thinking, and sustainable leadership, while highlighting opportunities to learn about climate change, social justice, and intersectionality. Open to all students, the program requires around 30 hours over the academic year, including workshops, self-directed learning, and reflection. On completion, students earn the Climate and Sustainability Leadership Edinburgh Award, which is added to their Higher Education Achievement Report, giving them practical skills and recognition for contributing to positive climate and sustainability outcomes.

The Edinburgh Award (Work Experience)

The Edinburgh Award (Work Experience) allows students to gain recognition from the University for skills developed during work experience outside their degree. The program is self-led, running from October to May, where participants set three development goals, reflect on their progress, and produce a written summary of their achievements. This flexible award helps students build practical skills, reflect on learning, and receive accreditation for work experience, providing a structured way to showcase professional development alongside their studies.

Go Abroad Fund

The Go Abroad Fund provides financial support for students to undertake a short-term international experience during summer 2026. Eligible students can use the fund for volunteering, internships, conferences, cultural activities, research, and other educational trips. Applications close Monday, 9 March 2026 at 12pm GMT.

INCiTE Summer School

A two-week, in-person summer course in Southeast Asia where students learn intercultural competence, social innovation, human-centered design, and entrepreneurship. Participants work in teams to build a business model, pitch solutions, and join a multicultural student community. Applications open 2 Feb 2026 and close 9 March 2026.

NICE Programme

The Network for Intercultural Competence to facilitate Entrepreneurship (NICE) offers virtual team-based learning on entrepreneurship and intercultural competence. Students develop business ideas to solve global challenges and can attend an optional summer school. The program runs March to June with no participation fees. Applications close 3 Feb 2026.
